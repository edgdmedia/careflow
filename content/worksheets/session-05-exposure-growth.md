# Session 5: Exposure & Growth

## Why Avoidance Keeps Anxiety Alive
Avoidance can bring short-term relief, but it often teaches your brain that the feared
situation is dangerous. Gentle, supported exposure helps your nervous system learn that
you can stay present and cope.

## Your Avoidance Pattern
- What situations do you avoid because of anxiety?
- What do you fear will happen?
- What does avoidance cost you?

## Start Small
Choose one low-to-moderate item from your ladder. The aim is not to overwhelm yourself.
The aim is to practice staying present long enough to learn that anxiety can rise and fall.

## Exposure Reflection
After the exercise, write:
- What did I predict would happen?
- What actually happened?
- What did I learn about myself?

## Compassion Reminder
Exposure is not about forcing yourself. It is about building trust in your ability to cope.
